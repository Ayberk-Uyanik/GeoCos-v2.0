Milkov's Approach

The most recent table-based method has been proposed in 2015 by Alexei Milkov. The advantage of this method is to suggest a single CoS value for each petroleum system element. With extensively detailed options, it is applicable for all basins.

Malvic's Approach

This method has been proposed in a 2009 paper named as "Stochastical approach in deterministic calculation of geological risk - theory and example". It is a table-based cos calculation method focusing on Drava depression of Croatia. However, it can be used for all hydrocarbon basins as well.





























CCOP's Approach

CCOP stands for "The Coordinating Committee for Coastal and Offshore Geoscience Programmes in East and Southeast Asia". Since 1987, it is an independent intergovernmental organisation carrying out activities in energy, mineral, coastal zone and geohazards sectors. One of its' working groups has proposed risk assessment table in 2000. The tables suggest a risk range for each petroleum system element.
